Part One — The Craft of the Prompt
Warm-up · The one rule

Vague in, vague out.

Treat Claude like a brilliant new hire on day one: it has zero context on your business. The clearer and more specific you are, the better it performs.

⟶ Vague

"Write me a social post about my candles."

⟶ Specific

"Write a warm, no-hype Instagram caption for my hand-poured soy candles, aimed at first-time buyers, under 60 words, ending with 'shop the spring set.'"

Source: Anthropic — "provide clear, explicit instructions with all the necessary detail," in Prompt engineering for business performance.

Why bother · Structure

A framework is a recipe
for a good prompt.

You already know precise is better. A framework just makes precise repeatable — so you stop reinventing the prompt every time. It buys you two things:

⟶ Speed

Once it's internalized, a strong prompt takes ~30 seconds instead of 5 minutes of staring at the box.

⟶ A quality floor

Even a quick prompt built on a framework hits the minimum context, specificity, and structure to get a usable answer.

Source: SurePrompts — The Best AI Prompt Frameworks (2026).

The toolkit · Four you'll actually use

Four frameworks, four jobs.

From a quick one-liner to a full launch plan — pick the one that fits the size of the task.

01 RTF Role · Task · Format Quick, well-defined tasks. Your everyday default.
02 CARE Context · Action · Result · Example Content that sounds like you — tone and style matter.
03 BAB Before · After · Bridge Sales & promo copy that moves people to buy.
04 RISEN Role · Instructions · Steps · End goal · Narrowing Big projects — and your operations agents.
Framework 01 · The quick one

RTF

Best for: quick, well-defined tasks — the fastest framework to reach for.
Template in action

Role: You're my email copywriter.
Task: Write a subject line + 3-sentence email for my 20%-off spring sale (hand-poured candles, runs Mar 15–22).
Format: Give me 5 subject-line options, then the email under 75 words.

RRoleWho Claude should be — "an email copywriter," "a brand strategist."
TTaskExactly what to do, in one specific, actionable request.
FFormatThe shape of the output — a list, a table, word count, tone.

Source: SurePrompts — RTF is ideal for routine content and quick one-off tasks.

Framework 02 · The storyteller

CARE

Best for: case studies, testimonials, campaigns — when it has to sound like you.
Template in action

Context: I run a Pilates studio; I want a client success story for my site.
Action: Write a short case study.
Result: A warm, 150-word story that builds trust with first-timers.
Example: [paste a testimonial in the voice you love].

CContextThe background and situation Claude is writing into.
AActionThe specific thing to produce.
RResultThe outcome you want — length, feel, what it should achieve.
EExampleA sample that locks in the style and cuts the vagueness.

Source: Virtasant — CARE is great for case studies, project reviews, and situations.

Framework 03 · The persuader

BAB

Best for: ads, promos, sales pages, launch emails — copy that moves people to buy.
Template in action

Before: My customers feel scattered — sticky notes everywhere, missed client follow-ups.
After: They run their whole week from one calm planner and never drop a client again.
Bridge: Write an Instagram ad for my "Weekly Reset" planner that connects the two, ending with "grab yours."

BBeforeThe customer's pain right now — the problem they feel.
AAfterThe happier place they want to be once it's solved.
BBridgeYour product or service as the path from one to the other.

Source: ChceDoInternetu — BAB is the most powerful framework for persuasive writing — marketing copy, sales emails, landing pages.

Framework 04 · The heavy lifter

RISEN

Best for: complex, multi-step work where the steps — and the limits — matter.
Template in action

Role: Act as a launch strategist.
Instructions: Build my 4-week launch plan for a new coaching program.
Steps: 1) define the offer · 2) content calendar · 3) email sequence · 4) launch-week schedule.
End goal: 25 enrolments.
Narrowing: Instagram + email only · $0 ads · keep my warm, no-hype voice.

↳ Same skeleton powers your operations agents in hour two — Role, Steps & Narrowing are exactly what an agent's standing instructions need.

RRoleThe persona and expertise to adopt.
IInstructionsThe task — what you want done.
SStepsThe exact sequence to follow.
EEnd goalWhat success actually looks like.
NNarrowingThe boundaries — what to avoid or leave out.

Sources: AiPromptsX — RISEN (by Kyle Balmer) is built for complex, multi-step tasks; on agents, see The RISEN Framework for AI Agents (dev.to).

The decision · Match it to the task

When to use each.

Start with RTF for most things. Climb the ladder only when the task gets bigger.

RTF A caption, a quick email, a summary. Quick
CARE When it has to sound like you — a story, a campaign. Style-driven
BAB Sales & promo copy that moves people to buy. Persuasion
RISEN A launch plan, an SOP, your operations agents. Complex build
← Simple & fastDetailed & structured →

Before we build · A few more dials

Choose your model. Choose your effort.

Two quick dials decide how smart, how fast, and how much of your usage each answer costs — plus two built-in tools for live info.

Model Haiku fast & light · Sonnet balanced · Opus heaviest reasoning. Pick the smallest that does the job.
Effort How hard it thinks. Low/Med faster, fewer tokens · High is default · Max only for hard, long tasks.
Web Search Quick, current info mid-chat — a lookup or two when you need today's facts.
Research A multi-step deep dive across many sources, returned as a cited report.

↳ Today: we'll run on Haiku at low–medium effort — snappy, and it saves your usage for the build hour. Step up to Sonnet or Opus when the task gets heavy.

Source: Anthropic — a control beside the model selector lets you choose Claude's effort; higher thinks more deeply, lower is faster and uses limits more slowly — available on all plans.

Building block 01 · Connectors

Give Claude access.

A connector is a one-click bridge to a tool you already use, toggled on per chat. Suddenly Claude can read your inbox, your calendar, your files.

⟶ Before

"What's important in my inbox today?" → "I can't see your inbox."

⟶ Connected

Same question → a real triage of your actual emails, ranked by what needs you first.

↳ Claude only sees what you authorize, and never sends, posts, or pays without showing you first.

Source: Claude Help Center — Claude connects to services like Google Drive, Gmail, Slack, DocuSign, and many more.

Your turn · ~2 minutes

Connect & confirm.

✦ Do this now
Add your Gmail or Calendar connector.

Then ask Claude one thing only it could answer — like "What's on my calendar this week?"

Check: did Claude pull up something only you can see? Then it's connected. If it says it can't, you just found the toggle.

Building block 02 · Skills

Teach it once.

A skill is a small instruction file that teaches Claude how to do one job your way. Write it once; it runs every time — by name with /skill, or automatically when the task matches.

⟶ Connector

Gives Claude access — what it can reach.

⟶ Skill

Gives Claude know-how — how to do the job, so you stop re-explaining it.

Source: Product Talk — Claude on the web (Claude.ai) and Claude Desktop also support skills — define once, use everywhere.

Skills · Build your own

Your morning routine, on autopilot.

Everybody has a morning. Let's make a skill that runs yours — using the calendar and email you just connected.

  1. Check my calendar and email for today.
  2. Flag the one thing I really need to handle.
  3. Give me a 5-line summary of both, in one place.
  4. Add one line to start the day right.
  5. Pull a headline or two I'd want to know about.

↳ Build it: ask Claude to "create a skill called morning-routine" → install it under Customize → Skills → run it with /morning-routine. Schedule it, and it's waiting when you wake up.

Source: How to AI — create the skill, install it under Customize → Skills, then "Try in chat".

Your turn · ~4 minutes

Check: did Claude run your whole routine from one command, using your real calendar and inbox? You just built a skill. Compare with the person next to you.
