Part One — The Craft of the Prompt
Warm-up · The one rule

Vague in, vague out.

Treat Claude like a brilliant new hire on day one: it has zero context on your business. The clearer and more specific you are, the better it performs.

⟶ Vague

"Write me a social post about my candles."

⟶ Specific

"Write a warm, no-hype Instagram caption for my hand-poured soy candles, aimed at first-time buyers, under 60 words, ending with 'shop the spring set.'"

Source: Anthropic — "provide clear, explicit instructions with all the necessary detail," in Prompt engineering for business performance.

Why bother · Structure

A framework is a recipe
for a good prompt.

You already know precise is better. A framework just makes precise repeatable — so you stop reinventing the prompt every time. It buys you two things:

⟶ Speed

Once it's internalized, a strong prompt takes ~30 seconds instead of 5 minutes of staring at the box.

⟶ A quality floor

Even a quick prompt built on a framework hits the minimum context, specificity, and structure to get a usable answer.

Source: SurePrompts — The Best AI Prompt Frameworks (2026).

The toolkit · Three you'll actually use

Three frameworks, three jobs.

RTFQuick asks
RRole — who Claude should be
TTask — exactly what to do
FFormat — the shape of the output

"You're my email copywriter. Write my spring-sale email. 5 subject lines, then 75 words."

CAREOn-brand content
CContext — the background
AAction — the thing to produce
RResult — length, feel, outcome
EExample — a sample in your voice

"I run a Pilates studio [C]. Write a client story [A]: warm, 150 words, builds trust [R]. Here's one I love: … [E]"

RISENBig builds & agents
RRole — the persona
IInstructions — the job
SSteps — the sequence
EEnd goal — what success is
NNarrowing — the boundaries

"Launch strategist [R]: build my 4-week plan [I], steps 1–4 [S], 25 enrolments [E], IG + email only, $0 ads [N]."

← Easiest · quick asksMost structured · big builds →

↳ Start with RTF for most things. Reach for CARE when it must sound like you. RISEN is for big builds — it's also the skeleton of the dashboard you'll build in hour two.

Sources: SurePrompts — RTF for routine, quick one-off tasks · Virtasant — CARE for case studies & reviews · AiPromptsX — RISEN for complex, multi-step tasks.

Before we build · A few more dials

Model, effort, and two more tools.

Two quick dials decide how smart, how fast, and how much of your usage each answer costs — plus two built-in tools for live info.

Model Haiku fast & light · Sonnet balanced · Opus heaviest reasoning. Pick the smallest that does the job.
Effort How hard it thinks. Low/Med faster, fewer tokens · High is default · Max only for hard, long tasks.
Web Search Quick, current info mid-chat — a lookup or two when you need today's facts.
Research A multi-step deep dive across many sources, returned as a cited report.

↳ Today: exercises run on Haiku — snappy and light. For the build hour, switch to Sonnet: digging through your files (bank statements, Instagram exports) takes real reasoning, and Sonnet is the right tool for it.

Source: Anthropic — a control beside the model selector lets you choose Claude's effort; higher thinks more deeply, lower is faster and uses limits more slowly — available on all plans.

Part Two · Beyond the prompt

Connect. Then teach.

Prompting is how you talk to Claude. These two building blocks give it hands and know-how — and they stack into everything you'll build.

ConnectorAccessPlug Claude into the tools you already use — email, calendar, files.
SkillKnow-howTeach it to do one job your way, every time — and run it by name.
Building block 01 · Connectors

Give Claude access.

A connector is a one-click bridge to a tool you already use, toggled on per chat. Suddenly Claude can read your inbox, your calendar, your files.

⟶ Before

"What's important in my inbox today?" → "I can't see your inbox."

⟶ Connected

"3 of 14 need you today: ① Sarah (client) — wants the invoice re-sent before Friday, I've drafted the reply. ② Maison Ralia — confirming your 2pm Thursday walkthrough. ③ Stripe — your $1,240 payout landed. The rest is newsletters and receipts."

↳ Claude only sees what you authorize, and never sends, posts, or pays without showing you first.

Source: Anthropic — Claude connects to your tools and data — Gmail, Calendar, Drive, and many more — via connectors.

Your turn · ~2 minutes

Connect & confirm.

✦ Do this now
Add your Gmail or Calendar connector.

Then ask Claude one thing only it could answer — like "What's on my calendar this week?"

Rather not connect your own inbox? Use our demo account: arcaidworkshops@gmail.com · password: letsplay! — it's a shared practice account, so don't put anything personal in it.

Check: did Claude pull up something only you can see? Then it's connected. If it says it can't, you just found the toggle.

Building block 02 · Skills

Teach it once.

A skill is a small instruction file that teaches Claude how to do one job your way. Write it once; it runs every time — one command, whole routine. Here's one in action:

/client-recap →
Step 1 · CalendarFind the meetingtoday's client call, via your connector
→
Step 2 · InboxPull the threadthat client's recent emails
→
Step 3 · WriteDraft the recapin your voice, ready to send
→
Step 4 · OrganizeList action itemswith owners & dates
↓
What comes back — every time, same shape

"Recap drafted for Sarah (2pm call): scope confirmed, launch moved to the 22nd. Action items: you — send revised quote (Fri); Sarah — final copy (Mon). Ready to send?"

Source: Product Talk — Claude on the web (Claude.ai) and Claude Desktop also support skills — define once, use everywhere.

Your turn · ~5 minutes

Build your morning routine.

Everybody has a morning. Describe yours; Claude turns it into a skill that runs on the calendar and email you just connected.

  1. Check my calendar and email for today.
  2. Flag the one thing I really need to handle.
  3. Give me a 5-line summary of both, in one place.
  4. Add one line to start the day right.
  5. Pull a headline or two I'd want to know about.
✦ Do this now
Ask Claude: "create a skill called morning-routine" — then describe your steps.

Install it under Customize → Skills and run it with /morning-routine. Make it yours — swap the quote for a to-do count, add your industry's news. Then schedule it so it's waiting every morning.

Check: did your whole routine run from one command, on your real calendar and inbox? You just built a skill. Compare with your neighbour — no two will match.

Source: How to AI — create the skill, install it under Customize → Skills, then "Try in chat".
